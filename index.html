Smarter VR Processing

Orbit 360 solves critical hurdles in the VR editing pipeline. Work with raw camera files, preserve spatial audio, and verify metadata formats with a clean, lightweight interface.

Multi-Format Support

Read native camera files (like GoPro .360 and Insta360 .insv) or standard exports, and transcode into MP4, M4V, MOV, Matroska (MKV), or WebM containers.

Preserve or Downmix Audio

Retain full 4-channel spatial sound fields (Ambisonics) in AAC format, mix down to clean stereo, or keep the original uncompressed audio track intact.

No Video Re-Encoding

When restoring spatial audio, Orbit 360 copies the video stream 1:1. This guarantees zero quality loss and yields blazing-fast processing speeds.

Hardware Acceleration

Leverages Apple VideoToolbox (H.264 & HEVC/H.265 hardware encoders) for optimal encoding speeds on macOS with minimal CPU overhead.

Batch Queue

Drag and drop up to 20 videos at once. Queue them up with custom parameters for output codec, resolution, quality, or framerate, then batch transcode them in one click.

Integrated VR Validator

Automatically validates outputs using ffprobe to confirm spatial layout, audio channels, and equirectangular projection tags before you upload to YouTube VR or Meta Quest.

Explore the Core Workflows

Orbit 360 operates in two primary modes designed to address the main pain points of 360° VR content creation.

Convert 360° Video

Transcode camera footage and master files with extensive control over video encoding, resolution scaling, frame rates, and audio mapping.

  • Video Codecs: Hardware-accelerated H.264 (VideoToolbox), HEVC (VideoToolbox), ProRes 422, VP9, or copy directly without re-encoding.
  • Scaling & Resolution: Same as source, 8K (7680x3840), 5.7K (5760x2880), 5K, 4K, 3K, 2K, or fully custom resolutions.
  • Audio Handling: Downmix to Stereo (AAC), preserve 4-channel Ambisonics (AAC at up to 768 kbps), keep raw audio streams, or remove audio completely.
  • Framerate Control: Keep source rates or target film-standard 23.98 fps, 29.97 fps, 50 fps, or 60 fps.

Restore Spatial Audio

Often, video editors (like Premiere or Resolve) compress spatial audio to stereo or strip VR tags when exporting graded masters. This mode solves this by merging the raw audio back into your finished edit.

  • 1:1 Video Stream Copy: The finished, graded video is copied directly without re-encoding—preserving 100% of the video quality and completing in seconds.
  • Spatial Sources: Import raw camera files, WAV, AAC, or M4A audio streams that contain the 4-channel spatial sound field.
  • Flexible Mapping: Choose to replace, keep, or pair the original ambisonic track alongside existing tracks.
  • VR Tags Intact: Existing equirectangular metadata is automatically detected and preserved.

Instant VR Validation

Don't waste hours uploading bad files. When a job finishes, Orbit 360 immediately probes the output file and displays a post-export checklist to guarantee full VR compatibility.

  • Checks projection metadata: Verifies correct "equirectangular" tagging for VR players.
  • Checks audio stream properties: Audits channel count (4 channels) and maps the layout.
  • Confirms file containers and stream alignments.
  • Warns of potential configuration errors immediately in the UI.

Quick Installation

Orbit 360 requires macOS 13 Ventura or newer. It uses the powerful FFmpeg engine under the hood to perform high-speed conversions.

1

Install FFmpeg

The easiest way is to install via Homebrew. Run this command in Terminal:

brew install ffmpeg
2

Download & Run Orbit 360

Download the latest release, drag Orbit 360 to your Applications folder, and configure path overrides in the app Settings if necessary.

Download Orbit360.dmg

Bundled Binaries

Are you deploying/redistributing or prefer static binaries without installing Homebrew?

You can bundle FFmpeg directly inside the application folder before packaging.

# Download static macOS binaries
./scripts/download_ffmpeg_macos.sh

# Compile universal app bundles
swift build -c release --arch arm64
swift build -c release --arch x86_64

# Package Orbit360.dmg
./create_app.sh
